MUNA

为喀麦隆医护人员设计的 AI 辅助宫颈癌筛查窥镜——从概念到可工作原型

Date 2024
Duration 6 months
Category Medical Product Design
Tools Solidworks, Raspberry Pi, Python, 3D Printing
My Role Hardware prototyping, light testing, Raspberry Pi integration
Team Group Project @ TU Delft

Background

In Cameroon, only ~4% of women have been screened for cervical cancer. Healthcare workers want to use AI-assisted diagnosis, but existing equipment produces poor image quality — high noise, strong reflections, and reliance on cell phones for image capture.

How can we redesign the speculum to help physicians capture high-quality cervical images for both diagnosis and AI dataset building?

System Vision

MUNA is a gun-shaped scanning device with dual cameras, integrated LED lighting, and a dedicated screen — replacing the unhygienic phone-based workflow with a purpose-built medical tool.

Design Iteration

Multiple rounds of handle and camera-head prototyping — from shape exploration to functional refinement.

Light Engineering

A key technical challenge: reducing tissue-fluid reflections while maintaining sufficient illumination. We tested diffusion materials and polarizer configurations, then optimized the light source position inside the speculum.

Final Design

Working Prototype

Functional prototype built with Raspberry Pi, achieving the full pipeline: button control, dual-camera switching, LED lighting, and live display on screen.
